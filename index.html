Liver vessel segmentation dataset
LiVS

Liver vessel segmentation is to pinpoint out the composing pixels of vessels from a medical image generated by computed tomography (CT), magnetic resonance imaging(MRI), computed tomography angiography (CTA), etc; see the follow figure. This task has increasingly importance in diagnosis formulating, surgical planning and radiotherapy.

LiVS is a fine-grained hepatic vascular dataset constructed by Dr.Zhao Liang's team. This dataset has a large number of volumes and high-quality masks. The LiVS is composed of 325 volumes and 9,983 CT slices, and each volume retrieves approximately 30 slices. The vessels of each slice are delineated by three senior medical imaging experts and the final mask is their majority voting. Due to the pretty small size of vessels, the delineation of each vessel can be oscillating a lot easily. To handle this problem, the coincidence of each vessel among the three masks is calculated. In case the majority voting over any mask is smaller than 0.5, the vessel is highlighted and sent back to the three experts for further refinement. This procedure repeats until no inconsistence exists.

Each folder holds one patient's information, the 325 folders correspond to 325 different patients, which can be downloaded individually or conjointly. The table below provides information on the image, such as liver size (width, depth, height) . These folders are called “Case-number” (the number varying between 0 and 324). Each “Case-number” folder contains 3 sub-folders called “dcms_”, “livs_” and “labs_”. These folders respectively contain the anonymized patient CT image in DICOM format, the corresponding liver image in .nii format, the labelled image corresponding to the liver vessels in .nii format.
